Overview

Welcome to the Workshop on Language, Reasoning, and Agents for Physical Sciences at ICML 2026! We explore how agentic AI systems can reason across multiple stages of scientific workflows and collaborate with human scientists in language—with a specific focus on the physical sciences, including plasma physics, quantum and particle physics, astronomy, Earth and planetary sciences, multiphysics engineering, and chemistry.

While AI has proven valuable for isolated scientific tasks such as anomaly detection, surrogate modeling, and data-driven inference, these approaches typically operate as standalone components. This workshop investigates the next frontier: agentic systems that integrate reasoning, planning, and language to operate across entire scientific pipelines. Key questions include how to decompose scientific workflows into agentic tasks, how to incorporate physical laws and constraints, how to evaluate agents in open-ended scientific settings, and how to design effective human–AI collaboration for discovery.

Physics offers a uniquely rigorous testbed for agentic intelligence: it demands multistep logical reasoning, symbolic–numerical integration, and strict adherence to governing equations. We welcome early-stage ideas, open challenges, and critical perspectives from researchers across AI and the physical sciences.

Call for Papers

We invite extended abstracts (up to 4 pages excluding references) for contributed talks and posters. Submissions are encouraged in the following areas, but are not limited to:

Topics of Interest

  • Agentic Systems for Science: Designing agents for data curation, literature retrieval, hypothesis generation, experimental design, validation, and other scientific subtasks; integrating physical laws and constraints; human-centered and interpretable agent design.
  • Benchmarks & Evaluations: Datasets and evaluation protocols for agents in the physical sciences; characterizing limitations and failure modes; interpretability, diagnostics, and attribution tools.
  • Philosophy of AI-Driven Discovery: How AI-driven automation reshapes scientific discovery; underexplored research directions; visions for human–AI collaboration in science.
